# Limitations — Real-World Evidence Studio

> **Synthetic data disclaimer.** All findings in this application are derived
> from Synthea-generated synthetic records. They do not represent real patients,
> clinical outcomes, treatment effectiveness, drug safety, or incidence rates.

---

## 1. Synthetic data (Synthea)

All data is generated by the Synthea open-source patient simulator. Synthea
produces longitudinal EHR records that resemble real data structurally but are
not derived from any real individuals.

**Implication:** No descriptive statistic, association, or model output in this
application represents a real population finding. Do not use results to inform
clinical decisions, drug safety assessments, regulatory submissions, or public
health reporting.

## 2. GLP-1 prescribing in Synthea

Synthea's diabetes management module generates GLP-1 prescriptions, but the
prevalence, sequencing, and drug choice do not replicate any real-world
prescribing pattern or clinical guideline. Results cannot be used to compare
the real-world effectiveness or utilization of individual GLP-1 agents.

## 3. Text-based concept matching

Drug and diagnosis identification relies on case-insensitive substring matching
against Synthea description fields and SNOMED codes embedded in the CSV output.
This approach is **not equivalent to validated clinical phenotyping**. In real
RWE studies, concept sets are reviewed by clinical subject-matter experts and
validated against gold-standard cohorts.

**Implication:** Cohort membership in this application reflects Synthea's
simulation model, not any formally validated phenotype algorithm.

## 4. New-user design assumptions

The new-user design assumes the first GLP-1 record in the Synthea file is
truly a new initiation. Prior exposure before the Synthea observation window
cannot be detected, and the data does not model real pharmacy records that
would be needed to confirm new use.

## 5. Baseline observation window

The 365-day baseline window is approximated by the presence of at least one
encounter record before the index date. Synthea's observation period is
bounded by the simulation start date, so some patients may appear to have
shorter histories than expected.

## 6. Complete-case analysis

Missing values for HbA1c and BMI are common because Synthea only generates
these measurements when clinically indicated within the simulation. The
complete-case logistic regression excludes patients with missing covariates,
which may introduce selection bias even in the synthetic data.

## 7. No confounding adjustment beyond regression

The multivariable logistic regression adjusts for observed baseline covariates.
Unmeasured or unmeasurable confounders (provider prescribing patterns, patient
preferences, disease severity gradations not captured in Synthea) are not
modelled. No causal interpretation of the regression coefficients is appropriate.

## 8. Encounter-class classification

Synthea uses encounter classes (emergency, inpatient, ambulatory, etc.) that
are structurally similar to but not identical to CMS site-of-service
definitions used in real claims-based RWE studies. ED visit counts may not
align with real-world ED utilization research definitions.

## 9. No competing risks

Death is treated as administrative censoring. A competing-risks analysis (e.g.,
Fine-Gray sub-distribution hazard model for the time-to-ED outcome) is not
implemented in V1.

## 10. OMOP demonstration layer

The OMOP-aligned layer uses concept ID 0 for all codes that have not been
mapped through an official OHDSI vocabulary. It is provided for illustrative
purposes only and does not constitute a validated OMOP CDM extract. See
`docs/omop_mapping.md` for details.

## 11. Small-sample instability

With fewer than 500 Synthea patients, the enrolled GLP-1 cohort may be very
small (fewer than 50 patients is common). Logistic regression estimates are
highly unstable at this scale, and the application will emit explicit warnings
when diagnostic thresholds are not met. Use at least 1,000–2,000 Synthea
patients for a more meaningful demonstration.

## 12. Single-simulation data source

Results vary across Synthea simulation runs because each run is generated
with a different random seed. The application's audit trail captures the
exact input file manifest (name, size, SHA-256 hash) so that results from
different runs are not inadvertently compared.
